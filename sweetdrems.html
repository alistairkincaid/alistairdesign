Sweet DREMs

I started this project to explore REM (rapid eye movement) sleep and better understand its role on idea generation, communicated though a motion context. The project quickly became a methodology driven exploration into my own dreams during REM sleep over a 10 day period. I would recreate dream tests I had found by researchers and scientists in the field, such as Robert Stickgold's tetris experiment to see if actions before sleep can influence what I dream about. I would also practice healthly sleep and reading before bed. Each morning I would record my dreams and illustrate them in my personal style.

After 10 days of this process I was successful in developing new ideas from my own REM sleep, however what really stood out to me was the overall benefit of interpreting and reflecting on my dreams. I found that interpreting my dreams gave me insight into making better decisions and improving my own well being.

In order to gather insights from dreams, you must keep an eye out while your eyes are rapidly moving.

When turning this research into a motion peice I was excited to show the journey I had been on, I kept developing narratives that were fairly orthodox. As I originally had an experimental approach to this project it made sense to create an experimental animation. I animated the illustrated recording of my dreams. The final exhibition of this project was at Media Design School. I set up an area with bean bags and used projection mapping to display my work underneath a staircase to create the context of sleep.

Since the exhibition of this work at Media Design School, I developed the animated imagery and tested the project at a house party. This is a different context from sleep and was to test the functionality of this project in a party atmosphere and its relationship with the blaring Drum and Bass music. I aim to continue developing this project for more related events (continue projecting cool stuff for people).
