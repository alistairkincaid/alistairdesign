Auckland Live Idents

This was a brief from the Auckland Live performing art organisation, to showcase the performing arts within four idents. An ident is a short sequence that markets the broadcaster, it plays a few seconds before and after a TV program. In each of these Auckland Live idents we follow a geometric shape that reflects one of the arts. As the story develops, the shape transforms and grows into elements relating to that art. This shows the nurturing and communal essence of Auckland Live, a space where performers can grow and be supported. The mood and use of colour changes in a seasonal cycle. This drives the narrative as the subject grows.

The performing arts of each ident are music, theatre, dance, and circus. I recorded myself and others playing out these arts in front of a green screen. This was a fun and new challenge for me. I had to drag a keyboard, drum kit, unicycle and much more into the Media Design School studio and spent a day experimenting and gathering footage. Along with this green screen footage I used certain 2D and 3D shapes and accents to make links to these arts. For example, a circular shape represents the rippling effect of music and a diamond like shape represents the dynamic movement of a dancer.

I used this project as an opportunity to try new techniques and develop new skills. The project was a narrative about growth, I believe I also grew as a motion designer.
